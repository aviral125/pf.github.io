Case Study
Smart Bike Sharing App
Alternate solutions to Transportation problems in Indian Metropolitans
If you are a commuter or if you frequently travel with cars, you know how difficult it is to get to office or Home at peak hours.
You must have at somepoint stuck in a traffic jam and wondered if walking would have been faster. Many people in Indian Metropolitans feel the same.
Bengaluru
Kolkata
Delhi
Mumbai
Project Goal
Allow people to rent a nearby smart-bike instantly, go wherever they want and drop off the cycle at parking nearest to their destination, and making bike renting more accessible and increasing participation.
Result
This app will allows users to pick up and leave a bike at their convince. It provides an affordable means of shared transportation for convenient short urban trips while reducing congestion, and city’s carbon footprint.
Effect
Changes this app can bring
1.Reduce on-road car traffic in metropolitan cities
2.Reduce individual carbon foorprint and overall pollution
3.Provice affordable transportation service
4.Provide healthier transportation alternative to people
Research
For my preliminary research, I did extensive reading on existing bike sharing companies’s business model and conducted competitive analysis on their products.

Discussions with friends and relatives in metropolitans who use commute often helped me to understand possible user types, their problems with current system, and their need for Smart Bike sharing service.
I gathered and formalised my findings to analyse how important a user type is for this service. I built a graph to communicate my findings easily and to determine the importance of each user type.
Defining Product
The research phase made it easier to define a scope for the project. Here are the main tasks I want user to be able to do with this app of this app:
•     Find a bike on map quickly.
•     Get clear directions to the bike.
•     Access/Unlock the bike.
•     Find trip details and map during a trip.
•     End trip and Payment
Considering all the needs and features of different user types, I outlined the major components of the app.
In order to finalise the list of features, I need to know how familiarity/competence with application affects users needs and goals. There needs to be features for people who use the app multiple times a day and know their way around the day.
I defined a certain guideline for how I want the product to behave with users
Improving experience for intermediate/experienced users
People who commute once or twice a day will get familiar with the app very soon and will need shortcuts to increase efficiency.

Some feature should be introduced to quickly unlock bike and some feature to end the trip manually without touching the smarthpone.
Mapping User Journey
Beginner/New User
User journey for new user is the longest, as the most important aspect of experience for new user is ease of understanding and how quickly he is able to engage with the bike.
Intermediate User
User Journey for Intermediate user is similar to Beginner/New user in terms in early process, but the journey end in optimised for this user type, Users can manually lock the bike and pay later.
Expert User
User Journey for this user type is the shortest as this type is the busiest of all others and is most familiar with the app.
Creating Information Architecture
Even if research about breadth and depth suggest that first is better than second for memory and cognitive load, I chose to reduce the breadth of this application by grouping features together, even if it meant creating unequal feature distribution.
Converting concepts into UI
Creating Wireframes
Once I got a better understanding of what I wanted to do and the issues I should tackle, I took some time to sketch layouts and try different design approaches. I like to draw low fidelity layouts as they help me determine very quickly what works and what doesn’t. I can iterate easily.

It also allows me to see the different versions I’ve worked on and in what direction I’m going. Sketches are also a good way to gather feedback before jumping ahead to detailed wireframes.
Finalizing UI
Once I was satisfied with wireframes I moved on to making UI on Sketch, I started with design of small elements and moved to screens. Each element was assigned a visual hierarchy and colours were selected appropriately.

Complete user journey can be broken down into four parts
1.  Finding a bike
2.  Unlocing a bike
3.  Ongoing Trip Details
4.  Payment Screen
Finding a Bike
Design of these screens are versy simple, main navigation is to be designed but in a way that does not encourage people not doing so doing the same.

Homegage and navigation bikes falls under this category of screens.
These screens remains the same for any user type be it expert user type or beginner/new user type.
Unlocking a bike
Interactiong with and unlocking a bike in a very important touchpoint in the complete process, ‘Unlock bike button’ activates when user is very close to bike location.
Experienced users may already know the location of bike and my not always need step by step direction to the bike loaction. There may also be cases of GPS not functioning properly.

QR Scanner Shortcut is available on homepage, and will be user by both intermediate and expert users.
Ongoing Trip Details
Trip start page starts normally with stats at zero and user can take the bike where ever they want. Ongoing trip shows trip duration amd calories consumption, this screen is alternate way of showcasing this service’s advantages
Payment Screen
Among all the steps, payment takes up most of the time and needs to be customised for different user type. I have proposed three payment methods for each user type based on usage.
1.
Less than once a weekThese users have the normal payment plan where they pay after each use.
2.
Twice or thrice a weekThese users have the option to pay after every use and also to pay later for two to three rides combined, this way user needs to pay only once a week.
3.
Daily usersThese users have the option to get monthly subscription for fixed amount, that allows them to use the bikes without worrying about payment at all.

Converting New users into intermediate users
One of the primary aim of the app was to convert new users into intermediate users, to accomplish this task new users should be educated about shortcuts and additional functionality after they have used the app four to five times.
Features to be introduced
1.
Inform user that manually locking the bike automatically ends the trip, and do so right after the user used his/her phone to end trip. This way user immediately recognizes value of this feature.
2.
Inform user of the QR Code scanner shortcut, this way user will have no problem even when GPS fails and at other times it makes the process faster for the users
